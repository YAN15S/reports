Objections Map

  • 1. Price -- "$39.95 seems expensive"

    Response: ~$1.30/day. Half of LMNT ($2.25-$2.50) and less than Liquid IV. Use a cost-per-day comparison in the ad or lander.
  • 2. Skepticism -- "I've tried electrolytes and they didn't work" (PRIMARY)
    Fear: Another overpriced supplement that changes nothing.
    Response: Synthetic magnesium oxide absorbs at under 10%. Coconut water minerals absorb differently. The mechanism they tried before was different.
  • 3. Relevance -- "I'm not an athlete"
    Fear: This is not made for me.
    Response: Everyday people need electrolytes more than trained athletes who drink intentionally all day. The 3pm crash is a non-athlete problem. Angle #1 speaks directly to this.
  • 4. Trust -- "Never heard of MicroIngredients"
    Fear: Unknown brand = quality risk.
    Response: 2,455 Amazon reviews, 4.2 stars, third-party tested formula. Let the reviews anchor trust before the brand name.
  • 5. Timing -- "I'll try later"
    Fear: Not urgent enough to act now.
    Response: "You've probably felt the 3pm drag for months." Urgency through accumulated cost of inaction, not scarcity tactics.
  • 6. Alternatives -- "I'll just drink more water"
    Fear: I can solve this for free.
    Response: More water without minerals flushes existing electrolytes. Angle #11 ("Water Isn't Hydrating You") pre-empts this directly.
  • 7. Risk -- "What if the taste is terrible?"
    Fear: Wasting $40 on something undrinkable.
    Response: Coconut water base = lighter, more natural, no metallic aftertaste (the documented complaint vs. LMNT). Lead with taste angle in UGC: "surprisingly mild."
