Summary

Customer Avatar

Male, 35-55, 10-22 handicap recreational golfer. Weekend warrior with a full-time job and family. Plays 18-30 rounds per year, can't practice mid-week. Has bought cheap putting mats before that curled, felt like carpet, and built the wrong habits. His identity is wrapped up in being a "good golfer" - and chronic 3-putting is the most humiliating part of his game. He wants to be respectable in his foursome without spending thousands on lessons or green fees.

The range is the comfortable habit that keeps handicaps stuck. Calling this out directly challenges the golfer's current behavior and makes him question everything.


Sample Hook

"Stop going to the driving range. 60% of your golf shots happen within 6 feet of the hole. You are spending $25 and 2 hours getting better at the 40% that matters less. Here's where your strokes are actually hiding."

Best Execution Format

Static with bold text or short UGC video. Open with the provocative headline "Stop going to the driving range" - then back it with the 60% stat. The contrarian frame demands attention because it tells the golfer he has been wrong this whole time. High-engagement format: will generate comments (agreement + disagreement) which amplify reach.

  • Dual hole sizes for progressive challenge
  • Visible muscle memory improvement after weeks of reps
  • Looks professional - suitable for home or office
