Research Notes

Top Pain Points (Grounded in Research)

1. Nighttime numbness (3am wakeup)
Carpal tunnel symptom that signals the pain has left the workplace and entered daily life. Highly specific, emotional, verified by multiple review sources.
2. Years of failed solutions
"I spent $800 on physical therapy before someone told me to change my mouse." Braces, PT, stretches, ergonomic breaks -- all tried, none fixed it. Average sufferer waits 2-4 years before finding the right tool.
3. Pain spreading beyond the wrist
Forearm, shoulder, neck involvement signals systemic deterioration. Users describe "the tension in my shoulder and arm is constant." Signals urgency.
4. Career and income threat
"If it is this bad at 30, I can only imagine by 50." Writers, coders, designers, marketers all face the same existential fear: the body that enables the career may not hold up.
5. The adjustment period fear
1-2 week adjustment curve is the #1 objection after price. Users who overcame it report 5-10 years of pain-free use. The adjustment is the final friction point before the transformation.
6. Multi-device switching friction
Professionals toggling between Mac + PC + tablet frustrated by mice that do not follow them. TECKNET's 3-device Bluetooth is a key differentiator here.
7. Wrist brace stigma at work
Wearing a brace in meetings signals health problems to colleagues and employers. Customers want a solution that is invisible -- and a vertical mouse is just a mouse.
8. Scroll wheel / click fatigue
Reviewers note scroll wheel and click mechanism durability -- pain during scrolling is specific and common. Separate from wrist positioning -- the button action itself can aggravate carpal tunnel.
9. Forced breaks destroying productivity
"I have to stop every 45 minutes because my wrist seizes up." Pain limits work output in a measurable way -- lost billable hours, missed deadlines, reduced quality of work.
10. Surgery as the feared endpoint
Carpal tunnel surgery averages $5,000+ and requires weeks of recovery. Most avatars have been told this is the "next step" -- profound fear and the strongest urgency trigger available.
